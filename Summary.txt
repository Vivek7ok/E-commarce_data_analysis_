1. OBJECTIVE
--------------------------------------------------------------------
The goal of this project is to analyze a real-world e-commerce
dataset (customers, orders, payments, products, reviews, and
sellers) to understand business performance and support
data-driven decisions in the areas of:

- Overall sales and revenue performance
- Customer behavior and spending patterns
- Product and category performance
- Seller performance and geographic distribution
- Delivery efficiency and order fulfillment
- Customer satisfaction (reviews) and payment behavior

The end result is a complete pipeline that takes raw, messy data
all the way through to a decision-ready Power BI dashboard.


2. PROCESS OF WORK (STEP BY STEP)
--------------------------------------------------------------------

STEP 1 - DATA COLLECTION
Raw data was sourced as 8 related CSV files (customers, orders,
order_items, payments, products, reviews, sellers, and a
category-name translation file), based on the public Brazilian
e-commerce dataset (Olist, via Kaggle).

STEP 2 - DATA CLEANING (Python / Pandas)
File: Python/Cleaing.py
- Loaded all 8 raw datasets using Pandas
- Orders table: missing order_approved_at,
  order_delivered_carrier_date, and order_delivered_customer_date
  were forward-filled; all date columns were converted from text
  to proper datetime format
- Reviews table: missing review_comment_title and
  review_comment_message were filled with placeholder text
  ("No Title" / "No Message") so the numeric review score was
  never lost
- Products table: missing product_category_name was filled with
  "Missing"; remaining missing values were filled with 0; numeric
  columns (description length, photo count, weight, dimensions)
  were converted to integer type
- Cleaned datasets were exported to CSV, ready for loading into
  the database

STEP 3 - DATABASE DESIGN & LOADING (PostgreSQL)
File: Python/Insert.py
- Cleaned CSV files were loaded into a PostgreSQL database
- A relational schema was built with proper primary keys
  (customer_id, order_id, product_id, seller_id, review_id) and
  foreign key relationships linking customers -> orders ->
  order_items -> products/sellers, plus payments and reviews
  linked to orders
- The full schema is documented as an ER diagram in the
  "ER Digram" folder

STEP 4 - SQL BUSINESS ANALYSIS (PostgreSQL)
Folder: Sql/ (8 scripts, 01 to 08)
Over 30 business questions were answered using SQL, covering:
  01_KPI_Analysis            - total revenue, total orders, AOV,
                                monthly revenue/order trends
  02_Customer_Analysis       - top states/cities, top spenders,
                                average spend, repeat customers
  03_Product_Analysis        - top categories by revenue and
                                volume, pricing, review scores
  04_Seller_Analysis         - top sellers, seller states,
                                seller review performance
  05_Order_Analysis          - order status breakdown, delivery
                                time, cancellations by month
  06_Payment_Analysis        - payment type revenue share,
                                payment type vs review score
  07_Review_Analysis         - review score distribution,
                                price vs review correlation
  08_Advanced_Window_Functions - running totals, highest revenue
                                month, top 10 orders by value

STEP 5 - EXPLORATORY DATA ANALYSIS (Python)
File: Python/EDA.py / EDA.ipynb
- Additional exploration and visualization in Python (Pandas,
  Matplotlib) to sanity-check the SQL findings and look at
  distributions, outliers, and correlations before building the
  dashboard

STEP 6 - DASHBOARD DEVELOPMENT (Power BI)
File: Power bi/E-commarce Analysis.pbix
A 6-page interactive dashboard was built:
  1. Executive Summary - total revenue, total orders, customer
     cities, total sellers, average review score, revenue by
     quarter, revenue by payment type, order status breakdown
  2. Sales - revenue trends, sales by category, orders by month,
     revenue share by payment type
  3. Customers - customers by city/state, average customer
     spend, repeat customers, revenue by state
  4. Products - category-level revenue and volume performance
  5. Delivery - delivery time and fulfillment performance by
     state
  6. Reviews - review score distribution and satisfaction trends
The dashboard uses year-level filters (2016/2017/2018) and
consistent cross-page navigation.

STEP 7 - DOCUMENTATION
- README.md, ER diagram, and workflow diagram were created to
  document the schema, process, and findings for anyone
  reviewing the repository.

4. KEY BUSINESS FINDINGS (based on actual data)
--------------------------------------------------------------------
- Total revenue: R$16.0 million across 99,441 orders
- Average order value (AOV): R$154.10
- Customer base: 96,096 unique customers served by 3,095 sellers
- 97% of orders (96,478) were successfully delivered; 625 were
  canceled
- Sao Paulo (SP) leads with 41,746 customers - more than 3x the
  next state (Rio de Janeiro, 12,852)
- Only ~3% of customers (2,997 of 96,096) placed more than one
  order - repeat purchase behavior is rare
- Top 5 revenue categories: bed_bath_table, health_beauty,
  computers_accessories, furniture_decor, watches_gifts - these
  same categories also lead in items sold
- Seller base is concentrated in Sao Paulo state (1,849 of 3,095
  sellers, about 60%)
- Average delivery time is 12 days 13 hours; northern states
  (Roraima, Amapa, Amazonas) take more than double the national
  average (26-29 days)
- Cancellations spike in specific months - August 2018 (84) and
  February 2018 (73) were the worst
- Credit card accounts for 78.3% of revenue, followed by boleto
  (17.9%), voucher (2.4%), and debit card (1.4%)
- Overall average review score is 4.09 out of 5
- Price has almost no correlation with review score
  (correlation approx -0.004) - charging more does not lead to
  better or worse reviews


5. BUSINESS RECOMMENDATIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------
1. IMPROVE CUSTOMER RETENTION
   Only about 3% of customers reorder. Introduce loyalty programs,
   post-purchase email/offer campaigns, and reminders to convert
   one-time buyers into repeat customers.

2. FIX DELIVERY TIMES IN THE NORTH
   Roraima, Amapa, and Amazonas have delivery times more than
   double the national average. Prioritize carrier partnerships
   or regional fulfillment hubs in these states.

3. INVESTIGATE SEASONAL CANCELLATION SPIKES
   August and February show unusually high cancellations.
   Review staffing, inventory, and carrier capacity around these
   periods to prevent repeat issues.

4. DOUBLE DOWN ON TOP-PERFORMING CATEGORIES
   Bed/bath, health & beauty, and computer accessories drive the
   most revenue and volume. Prioritize these in promotions,
   homepage placement, and inventory planning.

5. REDUCE SELLER CONCENTRATION RISK
   About 60% of sellers are based in Sao Paulo. Recruit and
   support sellers in other states to reduce single-region
   dependency and improve delivery times nationally.

6. EXPLORE HIGH-VALUE, LOW-VOLUME MARKETS
   States like Paraiba show a high average order value despite
   low order volume - a good target for focused marketing to
   grow volume in an already high-spending market.

7. FOCUS ON SERVICE QUALITY, NOT DISCOUNTING
   Since price does not affect review scores, invest in
   delivery speed and order accuracy rather than price cuts to
   improve customer satisfaction.


6. CONCLUSION
--------------------------------------------------------------------
This project demonstrates a complete, real-world data analytics
workflow: raw data was cleaned in Python, structured in a
PostgreSQL database, analyzed using SQL to answer over 30
business questions, and visualized in an interactive Power BI
dashboard. The findings point to a small number of high-impact
actions - improving retention, fixing regional delivery times,
and diversifying the seller base - that could meaningfully grow
revenue and improve efficiency without changing the underlying
product or market.
